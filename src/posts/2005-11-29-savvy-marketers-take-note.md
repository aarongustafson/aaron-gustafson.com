<p>One of the web’s preeminent marketing websites, <a href="http://www.marketingsherpa.com">MarketingSherpa</a>, has just published <a href="http://www.marketingsherpa.com/sample.cfm?contentID=3130">an article which may start a web standards stampede</a>. The focus is <a href="http://www.mozilla.org/firefox/">Firefox</a>, but the underlying message is standards, standards and more standards:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Your more savvy Web designers are likely all agog over Firefox, because its support of Web Standards makes it easier to design and maintain effective Web pages. …</p>

<!-- more -->

<p>For marketers, these standards are so darn important because they affect the bottom line: your budget. In fact, The Web Standards Project … estimates that before today’s growing lack of support for standards, the “fractured browser market” was adding at least 25% to the cost of developing Web sites. And that’s just one tiny piece of the revenue picture.</p>
<p><span class="initial quote">“</span>Housing construction, electrical wiring, automobile design, all these benefit from design standards,” says Scott McDaniel, MarketingSherpa’s own Internet Director. “Web site construction is maturing in much the same way.<span class="final quote">”</span></p>
</blockquote>
<p>The author, Heidi Anderson, even tallies her “6 Business Benefits of ‘Web Standards-based’ design”:</p>
<ol>
<li>Increased search engine optimization</li>
<li>Proper content presentation, including shopping carts and “contact us” forms</li>
<li>Decreased development and maintenance costs</li>
<li>Lower bandwidth usage</li>
<li>Faster download times</li>
<li>Web viewing beyond the computer (your site on wireless devices <span class="amp">&amp;</span> <span class="caps">RSS</span>)</li>
</ol>
<p>It’s nice to see marketers taking note of all we can do for them. Now roll up your shirt sleeves and get to work.</p>
