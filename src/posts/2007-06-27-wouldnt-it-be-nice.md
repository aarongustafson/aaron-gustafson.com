<p>Over the last two years, I’ve been wishing for just one thing in <span class="caps">CSS</span>: rotation. There’s been some discussion about it on the <abbr title="World Wide Web Consortium">W3C</abbr> lists, etc. but no one has made a solid pitch for it yet. Inspired a bit by <a href="http://www.stuffandnonsense.co.uk/archives/css3_multi-column_thriller.html">Andy’s modest <code>column-rule-image</code> proposal</a>, I drafted <a href="../../docs/CSS-rotation.pdf">a spec for <abbr title="Cascading Style Sheets"><span class="caps">CSS</span></abbr> 3 Rotation</a> [<abbr title="Portable Document Format"><span class="caps">PDF</span></abbr>] back in February. I showed it to a few folks at Web Directions North and got some good feedback.</p>
<p>Out of those discussions, I also realized we really needed a text-wrapping property in <abbr title="Cascading Style Sheets"><span class="caps">CSS</span></abbr> 3, so I also drafted <a href="../../docs/CSS-polygonal-margins.pdf">a spec for <abbr title="Cascading Style Sheets"><span class="caps">CSS</span></abbr> 3 Polygonal Margins</a> [<abbr title="Portable Document Format"><span class="caps">PDF</span></abbr>]. The idea is based on the polygons we used for image maps back in the day and would allow complete control over how text wraps around an element.</p>
<p>Anyway, I’ve sat on these for a while and I figured now was as good a time as any to unveil them to the world and solicit your feedback. So what do you think? Could you use this sort of control in your everyday <abbr title="Cascading Style Sheets"><span class="caps">CSS</span></abbr> work? How would you want to see it work? Do you think the plans I’ve outlined offer enough flexibility?</p>
