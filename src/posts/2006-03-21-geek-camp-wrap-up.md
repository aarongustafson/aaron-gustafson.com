<p>
	I had a fantastic time at SXSWi this year. It was great to catch up with old friends, make some new ones, and see what everyone’s been working on for the last year. If you’re interested in seeing the shennanigans you can check out <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/aarongustafson/sets/72057594078814094/">my SXSWi 2006 photostream</a>. Highlights are below:</p>
<p>
<strong><span class="initial quote">“</span>How to Bluff Your Way in <abbr title="Document Object Model"><span class="caps">DOM</span></abbr> Scripting<span class="final quote">”</span></strong> — Jeremy and I had a blast walking the audience through the wonderful world of <abbr title="Document Object Model"><span class="caps">DOM</span></abbr> Scripting. And, based on the audience feedback, we made quite an impact too. Hopefully we managed to break down some of the misconceptions about JavaScript and the <abbr title="Document Object Model"><span class="caps">DOM</span></abbr> being hard to work with. After all, <abbr title="Document Object Model"><span class="caps">DOM</span></abbr> Scripting doesn’t suffer from nearly as many compatibility issues as <abbr title="Cascading Style Sheets"><span class="caps">CSS</span></abbr>. There are some <a href="http://www.technorati.com/search/How%20to%20Bluff%20Your%20Way%20in%20DOM%20Scripting">great reviews and blow-by-blows</a> out there if you’re interested.</p>
<p>
<strong><span class="initial quote">“</span>Web Standards and Search/<span class="caps">SEO</span><span class="final quote">”</span></strong> — It was nice to finally get some dialogue going between the web standards community, the search engines and the <span class="caps">SEO</span> folks. It was great to have such incredible people working to make it happen too. Many thanks to Molly, Peter, Tim, Andy, Ed and Eric for putting in the time (and putting up with a little abuse) to get the ball rolling.</p>
