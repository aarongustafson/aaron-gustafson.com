<p>So, upon <a href="/notebook/geek-camp-wrap-up/">returning home from SXSW</a> and cracking open the new issue of <cite><a href="http://www.seedmagazine.com">Seed</a></cite>, I read something I wish I’d known before:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Having sex is the best way to prepare for a speech.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>How did I miss that? Where was I when that little nugget was passed out?</p>
<p>Prior to SXSW, <a href="http://www.mezzoblue.com">Dave</a> (and his many commenters) had given <a href="http://www.mezzoblue.com/archives/speaking_ti/">some great tips for giving memorable (and coherent) presentations</a>. I had kinda skimmed over it at the time, but, after re-reading it, I didn’t see <em>that</em> mentioned anywhere.</p>
<p>To make sure the clever editors at <cite>Seed</cite> were not simply pulling my leg (or some other appendage), I did a little digging and found that <a href="http://www.google.com/search?q=sex+%22public+speaking%22+%2Bstudy&amp;start=0&amp;ie=utf-8&amp;oe=utf-8">this story was everywhere in late January</a>. Where the hell was I?</p>
<p>Well, it settles one thing… Kel is definitely travelling with me everywhere I speak now ;-)</p>
