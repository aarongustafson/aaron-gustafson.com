<p>Eric has a very enlightened post <a href="http://meyerweb.com/eric/thoughts/2006/02/03/charting-ie7b2/">for those of you out to document bugs in <abbr title="Internet Explorer 7">IE7</abbr> Beta 2</a>. He also echoed my feelings that <em>this is a beta people!</em></p>

<!-- more -->

<blockquote cite="http://meyerweb.com/eric/thoughts/2006/02/03/charting-ie7b2/">
<p>Trying to fix a site that’s “broken” in <abbr title="Internet Explorer 7 Beta 2">IE7B2</abbr> is kind of like deciding to raze your profitable gas station just because you heard car companies are experimenting with hydrogen fuel cells. When the final version of <abbr title="Internet Explorer 7">IE7</abbr> comes out, <em>then</em> you can worry about what to do.  Maybe your site won’t be “broken” any more, and you won’t have to do anything.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>As Eric also mentioned, over on the <a href="http://blogs.msdn.com/ie"><abbr title="Internet Explorer">IE</abbr> Blog</a>, they’ve posted an entry about <a href="http://blogs.msdn.com/ie/archive/2006/02/02/523679.aspx">the current state of CSS fixes in <abbr title="Internet Explorer 7">IE7</abbr> and what you should be able to do now</a>. Also, for those of you who (like me) <a href="/notebook/a-load-of-malarkey/">uninstalled the beta</a> because it caused you to lose your <abbr title="Internet Explorer 6">IE6</abbr>, here are instructions on how to have both (found somewhere in the comments on <a href="http://www.digg.com">digg.com</a>):</p>
<ul>
<li>Extract <a href="http://go.microsoft.com/fwlink/?LinkId=58957">IE7B2P-WindowsXP-x86-enu.exe</a> to a temporary folder. (using <a href="http://www.rarlab.com/">WinRAR</a> or some such)</li>
<li>Working within the extracted directory, delete the following:          
<ul>
<li>update (folder),</li>
<li>install.ins,</li>
<li>spmsg.dll,</li>
<li>spuninst.exe, and</li>
<li>spupdsvc.exe</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Create a blank text file within the directory and name it “iexplore.exe.local<span class="final quote">”</span></li>
<li>Move the directory to a location in which you want to store the (now) standalone version of Internet Explorer 7 Beta 2 (e.g. Program Files)</li>
<li>Execute iexplore.exe</li>
</ul>
