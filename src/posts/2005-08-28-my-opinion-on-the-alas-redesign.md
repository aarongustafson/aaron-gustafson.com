<p>Yeah, I’m weighing into the debate on the <abbr title="A List Apart">ALA</abbr> redesign. I have to say I agree with Jon and Jeremy regarding the fixed 1024<abbr title="pixels">px</abbr> width. My little 2¢; to add to this discussion is that I think more designers should consider the wonderous world of CSS switching based on browser width (see <a href="http://www.rammstein.com">Rammstein</a> or the slightly better implementation on <a href="http://www.drink-drive-lose.com/adchallenge">Drink-drive-lose.com’s Ad Challenge</a>). Using this technique, users can view your site at their most comfortable screen resolution <em>and</em> you can still have a nicely designed page for them (fixed or liquid… or both). Can you say <a href="http://www.alistapart.com/articles/lowvision">zoom</a> <a href="http://www.stopdesign.com/log/2005/06/24/zoom-layout.html">layouts</a>? I knew you could.</p>
