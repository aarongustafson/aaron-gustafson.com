<p>With all of the travelling I was doing last week, I forgot to mention that some of the sites Dave <span class="amp">&amp;</span> I have worked at <a href="http://www.cronin-co.com">Cronin and Company</a> on took home <a href="http://www.webaward.com">WebAwards</a> from the <a href="http://www.webmarketingassociation.org/">Web Marketing Association</a>. We took home two “Outstanding Website” awards (think silver) for our sites for <a href="http://www.midhosp.org">Middlesex Hospital</a> and the <a href="http://www.drink-drive-lose.com/adchallenge/">Drink-Drive-Lose Ad Challenge</a>. I was perticularly stoked as I was the designer of both sites and single-handedly built the <a href="http://www.drink-drive-lose.com/adchallenge/">Ad Challenge</a> site which had a lot of complex application-level code behind it. We also took home a “Standard of Excellence” award (think bronze) for the site we built for Garelick Farms’ <a href="http://www.overthemoonmilk.com">Over the Moon Milk</a> product launch. I’m not a big fan of that site’s design (not because it wasn’t mine), but I love <a href="http://web.archive.org/web/20061103141015/www.overthemoonmilk.com/game/">the game Dave built</a>.</p>

<!-- more -->

<p>I was also a judge of the WebAwards (as I have been for the last few years and, no, I did not judge any sites I was involved with) and I have to say I was very pleased to see more sites moving to web standards. Out of the 15 or so I judged in the initial round, there were at least two or three that were well on their way to being exemplary in the standards world (compared to 0 only two years ago). Like most marketing-related awards, <a href="http://www.macromedia.com/software/flash/">Flash</a> always seems to trump standards, but it seems like developers are starting to cue into the standards trend more and more every year.</p>
<p>This year I also had the privilege of judging the Best of Show. I was pulling for <a href="http://web.archive.org/web/20080306081408/www.projectrebirth.org/">Project Rebirth</a> but National Geographic ended up taking home the top prize for <a href="http://channel.nationalgeographic.com/channel/mafia/">Inside the Mafia</a> which was also in my top 3 picks.</p>
<p>All in all, this years WebAwards were pretty good to us (and a step up from the two “Standard of Excellence” awards we pulled last year for <a href="http://www.ride4ever.org">Ride4Ever</a> and <a href="http://www.bertuccis.com">Bertucci’s Restaurants</a>). I look forward to next year’s competition as well as the one at <a href="http://www.sxsw.com"><span class="caps">SXSW</span></a>, where we hope to go from finalist to winner this year.</p>
