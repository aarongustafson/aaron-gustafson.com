<p>Microsoft released <a href="http://www.microsoft.com/windows/IE/ie7/ie7betaredirect.mspx">Internet Explorer 7 Beta 2</a> for public consumption yesterday. Based on everything I’d been reading, the development team seemed to be <a href="http://blogs.msdn.com/ie/archive/2005/07/29/445242.aspx">moving in the right direction</a>. I decided to take it for a test drive to see how things were coming along.</p>

<!-- more -->

<p><a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/aarongustafson/94150892/" title="A screenshot of And All That Malarkey in Internet Explorer 7 Beta 2. You can view this image in it's entirety on Flickr."><img alt="A screenshot of And All That Malarkey in Internet Explorer 7 Beta 2" class="feature" src="/i/posts/2006-02-02/a_lot_of_malarkey_with_ie1.png"/></a></p>
<p>The answer is “not well” I’m afraid. I took my first journey in the new browser over to <a href="http://www.stuffandnonsense.co.uk/">my friend Andy’s house</a>. I did this mainly because I love <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/aarongustafson/94250918/">how Andy handles <abbr title="Internet Explorer"><span class="caps">IE</span></abbr>. It’s beautiful</a>. I wanted to see if <abbr title="Internet Explorer 7"><abbr title="Internet Explorer 7"><span class="caps">IE</span>7</abbr></abbr> did the right thing and got the real design for And All That Malarkey. As it began rendering, my heart was racing and I was ecstatic to see the beautiful blues and reds of Andy’s blog coming through. Then, something a little odd happened. Somehow <abbr title="Internet Explorer 7"><abbr title="Internet Explorer 7"><span class="caps">IE</span>7</abbr></abbr> missed the boat and the page was rendered virtually unreadable (except the latest articles section) because a bunch of And All That Malarkey logos kept popping up everywhere.</p>
<p>Now according to Chris Wilson over at Microsoft (who’s team has been <a href="http://webstandards.org/press/releases/archive/2005/07/05/index.html">working closely with the wonderful folks at <abbr title="Web Standards Project">WaSP</abbr> to make <abbr title="Internet Explorer 7"><abbr title="Internet Explorer 7"><span class="caps">IE</span>7</abbr></abbr> standards-compliant</a>):</p>
<blockquote cite="http://blogs.msdn.com/ie/archive/2005/07/29/445242.aspx">
<p>Beta 1 makes little progress for web developers in improving  	our standards support, particularly in our <abbr title="Cascading Style Sheets"><span class="caps">CSS</span></abbr> implementation.  I feel badly about this, but we have been focused on how to get the most done overall for <abbr title="Internet Explorer 7"><span class="caps">IE</span>7</abbr>, so due to our lead time for locking down beta releases and ramping up our team, we could not get a whole lot done in the platform in beta 1. However, I know this will be better in Beta 2</p>
</blockquote>
<p>At the same time, I know Andy’s a stand-up guy and his <abbr title="Cascading Style Sheets"><span class="caps">CSS</span></abbr> is top notch, easily some of the best I’ve seen, this problem’s gotta lie with <abbr title="Internet Explorer 7"><abbr title="Internet Explorer 7"><span class="caps">IE</span>7</abbr></abbr>. I guess you can only expect so much from a beta (even a “beta 2”), but that’s a doosie of an error. At least <abbr title="Internet Explorer 7"><abbr title="Internet Explorer 7"><span class="caps">IE</span>7</abbr></abbr> was easy to uninstall and I’ve got some commentary to leave on the <a href="http://www.microsoft.com/communities/newsgroups/list/en-us/default.aspx?dg=microsoft.public.internetexplorer.general&amp;cat=en_us_28cca3eb-7037-4d4f-bde1-d8efee1f1420&amp;lang=en&amp;cr=us"><abbr title="Internet Explorer 7"><abbr title="Internet Explorer 7"><span class="caps">IE</span>7</abbr></abbr> Beta 2 feedback form</a>.</p>
